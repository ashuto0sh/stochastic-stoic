Every sprint planning meeting contains a moment of collective theater: eight
adults solemnly comparing invisible cards to decide whether a task nobody has
investigated is a 3 or a 5. The number will be wrong. Everyone knows the
number will be wrong. The ceremony proceeds anyway, because the ritual is not
about accuracy — it is about the *feeling* of control.

More here later: the log-normal distribution of task durations, why the
median lies, and what to do instead (reference class forecasting, or just
counting finished tickets).
